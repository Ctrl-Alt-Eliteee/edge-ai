## 🎯 Objective

The objective of today's lesson was to understand how Random Forest improves upon a single Decision Tree by combining the predictions of multiple trees. I also learned why randomness and diversity among trees make the model more robust and better at generalizing to unseen data.

---

## 🧠 What I Learned

- Learned that Random Forest is an ensemble of multiple Decision Trees.
- Understood why it is called "Random" and "Forest."
- Learned that each tree is trained on a different random subset of the training data.
- Understood how majority voting is used for classification problems.
- Learned that averaging predictions is used for regression problems.
- Compared Decision Trees and Random Forests in terms of accuracy, memory, speed, and interpretability.
- Learned that a higher accuracy model is not always the best choice, especially for Edge AI systems with limited resources.
